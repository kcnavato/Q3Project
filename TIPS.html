Indoor Fire Prevention Tips

  • Have a fire extinguisher ready incase of emergency.
  • Install smoke detectors in places that will most likely cause fire.
  • Be careful of flammable materials.
  • Don't smoke inside.
  • Always remember to turn off your stove or cooking appliances.
  • Have a fire escape plan.

Outdoor Fire Prevention Tips


  • Keep a 30-foot clearance around your home.
  • Dispose flammable materials properly.
  • Avoid using outdoor fires on windy days because it might spread and catch other stuff on fire.
  • Clear away stuff that easily catches on fire from your yard.
  • Keep a garden hose and fire extinguisher readily available incase of a fire.

What to do in case of a Fire

If a fire does occur, it's important to act quickly and calmly. Remember the following:

  1. Call the fire department and alert others in the building
  2. Exit the building immediately, using the designated fire escape route if possible
  3. If there's smoke, stay low to the ground because you might get suffocated.
  4. If your clothes catch on fire, stop, drop, and roll
  5. Don't go back into the building until the fire department has deemed it safe to do so
